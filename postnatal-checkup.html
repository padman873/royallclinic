When To Book

Early postnatal care helps prevent complications

Even if birth felt normal, a postnatal check is valuable to verify healing, check baby feeding, and support the early weeks after delivery.

Book a review if you notice

  • Heavy postpartum bleeding, pain, fever, or wound concerns.
  • Breastfeeding difficulties, sore nipples, or low milk supply.
  • Baby feeding issues, poor weight gain, jaundice, or unsettled behaviour.
  • Postpartum mood changes, anxiety, or difficulty coping with recovery.
  • Questions about contraception, sleep, or returning to activity safely.
Care Pathway

How the postnatal visit works

01

Review

The doctor checks mother and baby, asks about recovery, feeding, and any concerns.

02

Advise

Practical recommendations are given for feeding, wound care, rest, and baby support.

03

Treat

Treatments, wound care, or investigations are arranged if needed.

04

Follow-Up

A follow-up plan is provided for ongoing postpartum and newborn support.
