What is an Ingrown Toenail?

Painful nail growth into the skin

An ingrown toenail occurs when the nail edge grows into the surrounding skin, causing pain, swelling and sometimes infection. Left untreated, it can worsen and become more difficult to manage.

Common causes

Improper nail trimming, tight shoes, trauma, or naturally curved nails often cause the nail to press into the skin.

Signs to watch

Pain, redness, swelling, warmth, and occasionally pus are signs that an ingrown nail needs professional review.

When to seek care

If symptoms persist, worsen, or you have diabetes or poor circulation, prompt medical assessment is important to avoid complications.

Treatment Focus

Clinical relief and safe nail care

Royall Clinic provides careful assessment and treatment that may include nail trimming, dressing, infection control, or minor procedure if needed.

How we help

  • Assess the nail, surrounding skin, and infection risk.
  • Provide gentle cleaning, padding, and dressing.
  • Recommend or perform minor nail correction when clinically appropriate.
  • Advise footwear, hygiene, and follow-up care to reduce recurrence.
