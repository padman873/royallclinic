Procedure Overview

Planned removal with clinical care

Sebaceous cyst removal is best done under sterile conditions with a doctor review to reduce recurrence and support wound healing.

Assessment First

The doctor checks the cyst, surrounding skin, infection signs, and whether removal in clinic is appropriate.

Local Anaesthesia

Removal is performed comfortably with local anaesthetic so the cyst can be excised safely and cleanly.

Complete Excision

Where possible, the cyst sac is removed to reduce the chance of recurrence and allow optimal healing.

Wound Care

Post-procedure dressings, antibiotics if needed, and aftercare advice are provided for the best outcome.

Follow-Up

Review is available to check healing, remove sutures if needed, and answer any recovery questions.

Infection Prevention

Doctor-led care aims to minimise infection risk and manage inflamed or painful cysts safely.

When To Remove

Do not wait if it becomes painful or infected

Sebaceous cysts are often harmless, but removal is recommended when they enlarge, become tender, leak, or interfere with comfort.

Book a review if you notice

  • A growing lump that feels firm, mobile, or uncomfortable.
  • Redness, warmth, pain, or pus draining from the cyst.
  • A lump in a visible area causing cosmetic concern.
  • Repeated cyst inflammation or a cyst that returns after simple drainage.
  • Difficulty reaching or caring for the area safely at home.
