When To Book

Responsive care for baby congestion

Phlegm suction is a supportive service for infants with excess mucus, especially when feeding or breathing become difficult.

Consider a review if baby has

  • Persistent nasal blockage interfering with feeding.
  • Wet or rattly cough, difficulty clearing mucus.
  • Rapid or noisy breathing with mucus present.
  • Refusal to feed or unsettled sleep due to congestion.
  • Need for safe clinical support beyond home saline and suction.
Care Process

How infant phlegm suction works

01

Assess

The doctor checks baby’s breathing, congestion, feeding, and general condition.

02

Prepare

Baby is held gently and the airway is prepared with saline or humidification if needed.

03

Suction

Phlegm suction is done carefully with the appropriate device for infants.

04

Support

Aftercare advice is given for feeding, mucus management, and when to follow up.
