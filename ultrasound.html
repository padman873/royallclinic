Scan Uses

Imaging support for common clinical questions

Ultrasound uses sound waves to help visualise selected internal structures. Your doctor will decide whether it is the right test for your symptoms or screening needs.

Abdominal Review

May support assessment of abdominal pain, bloating, liver, gallbladder, kidney, or urinary concerns.

Pelvic Assessment

May be discussed for selected lower abdominal, menstrual, ovarian, uterine, or pelvic symptoms.

Pregnancy-Related Checks

Ultrasound may support early or antenatal review depending on timing, symptoms, and clinical need.

Soft Tissue Lumps

Selected lumps, swellings, cyst-like areas, or soft tissue concerns may be assessed with imaging.

Urinary Concerns

Doctor review may include ultrasound guidance for recurrent symptoms, kidney concerns, or bladder issues.

Result Explanation

Your doctor explains findings and whether medication, blood tests, repeat scan, or referral is needed.

Preparation

Some scans need simple preparation

Preparation depends on the body area being scanned. The clinic will advise if fasting, a full bladder, or timing matters for your scan.

Tell the clinic about

  • Your symptoms and how long they have been present.
  • Pregnancy status or possibility of pregnancy.
  • Previous scan reports, blood tests, or referral letters.
  • Whether the scan is urgent, routine, or for follow-up.
  • Severe pain, fever, bleeding, vomiting, or rapidly worsening symptoms.
