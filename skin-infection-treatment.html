Infection Types

Treatment depends on the cause

Different infections need different care. A doctor review helps decide whether antibiotics, antifungals, antivirals, drainage, dressings, or further tests are needed.

Bacterial Infection

Review for redness, warmth, swelling, pus, boils, cellulitis-like symptoms, or infected wounds.

Fungal Infection

Care for itchy, scaly, ring-shaped, moist, or recurring fungal rashes on skin, folds, feet, or nails.

Viral Skin Concerns

Assessment for wart-like lesions, blisters, painful eruptions, or suspected viral skin conditions.

Abscess & Boils

Painful pus collections may need medication, drainage assessment, wound care, or close follow-up.

Infected Eczema

Inflamed eczema can become infected and may need combined infection and dermatitis management.

Recurring Infection

Repeated infections may need trigger review, hygiene advice, glucose screening, or further investigation.

Warning Signs

Some infections need urgent care

Rapid spread, fever, severe pain, or infection in high-risk patients should not wait.

Seek review quickly if there is

  • Fever, chills, severe pain, or feeling very unwell.
  • Spreading redness, swelling, warmth, or red streaks.
  • Pus, abscess, blackened skin, or rapidly worsening wound.
  • Infection near the eye, face, genitals, hand, or joint.
  • Diabetes, poor circulation, immune suppression, or poor healing.
